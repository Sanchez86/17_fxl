Secrets of Perfect Momentum Trading

If you are new to Forex, most likely that you feel lost and confused in the world of trading. While professional traders strictly follow the rules and wait for the perfect setup, you may be the one who constantly changes his mind, and jumps ship in a hurry. If it is all about you, well, you are a perfect candidate for momentum trades. So for starters, what is momentum trade?

Investopedia defines a momentum strategy as the one that “seeks to profit from buying high and selling higher on the long side while selling low and covering lower on the short side”. It also says that “in momentum trading, traders focus on stocks that are moving significantly in one direction on high volume”. Simply saying, you make trading decisions based on visible market moves without deep analysis. And it can work for you if you follow the certain rules.

Seek out proper currencies and securities

Choose liquid assets as their price swings allow making right trading decisions. Try to select securities that are traded more than five million shares per day. Watch for news breakers, companies that launch new products or appear on the market and capture public’s attention. Small and mid-sized IT and innovative companies are perfect

Don’t be too greedy

Smart momentum trading involves tight risk control. Instead of choosing the most popular deals, pay attention to the most promising setups with a reward/risk setup of 3:1. We recommend using Bollinger Bands and other tools from technical analysis to define when the counter trend appears.

It’s all about perfect timing

In a perfect world, the best momentum trade happens when you notice the trend in the very beginning and make the fast move. Still, most traders don’t see the trading opportunity until it’s too late and they are the last ones to jump in the boat.

Perfect management

This stage is one of the hardest, it requires patience, experience, and knowledge. The longer positions you take the higher is the risk. Holding periods can vary. The idea is to exit when the trend slows down, so it is recommended to reduce position size if you are holding multiple positions.

Perfect exit

Firstly, there is no perfect exit strategy. Sometimes, you sell too soon. Sometimes, it is too late. The only thing that matters is that you should protect yourselves from significant losses. We recommend taking a blind exit when technical indicators hit the major high/lows.

We hope that these rules will make your momentum trading more profitable. Good luck!
