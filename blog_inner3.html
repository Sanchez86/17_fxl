5 Easy Steps to Trade Forex for Beginners

Jul 28, 2016

5 Easy Steps to Trade Forex for Beginners

When you are starting to trade Forex there are many things to learn. However, many traders try to skip this part and never go deep – they rather follow “the tips and tricks to make millions” than understand how the market really works. Forex trading is not as hard and complicated as many beginners think. Still, you would need some time and persistence to explore it. Here are a few things to put in place for a good start.

1. Know yourself

It may sound illogical, but your relationship with money in every-day life will eventually lead to the same result in the Forex market. So begin with examining your belief system: do you think that you need to struggle and work hard to make a penny? Have you lost money on a regular basis or lived from a paycheck to paycheck? Even if so, stay calm and try to change your belief system. As Henry Ford said: “Whether you think you can, or you think you cannot, you are right.”

2. One step at a time

This advice applies to every aspect of your life. Whether learn trading, change your mindset or chase a big dream, do not try to learn Forex in a week. Don’t panic when you see complicated charts and articles. Have a plan and make one step at a time. Start with a demo account, educate yourself, and make changes to your plan.

3. Never stop self-educating

This is probably the most important advice so far – read books on trading, dive deep to technical and fundamental analysis, read as much as you can about markets. Start your day with a portion of financial news. Financial education is a pillar to well-being so this background will pay itself eventually one day.

4. Analyze it!

This step comes naturally from the previous one. Try to analyze price movements, read company spreadsheets available online, explore market articles and analyst’s opinions. Try to implement your knowledge on fundamental and technical analysis.

5. Trade!

If you have reached sustainable success on a demo account, you can start live trading. Open a live account, deposit a reasonable amount and trade like you did on a demo account. When starting, so not try to become a full-time trader in a week, though take advantage of the market!

It's just not enough to decide become a trader. You need to hone your trading skills constantly and never stop learning. Hopefully, these five steps will give you a push to continue. Good luck!
