Things You Should Know To Short Sell Successfully

What is short-selling? To keep it simple, short selling is an opportunity to profit from a falling market. Successful short selling may work for you, but it’s a rare situation, as the majority of markets usually rise. Nevertheless, when markets do break below support levels, short-selling skills can make you millions. Yes, you’ve heard it right – millions of dollars! Just remember the legendary George Soros who made $1 billion profits back in 1992, when the British Pound Sterling was falling lower than its minimum. That day was called black Wednesday – a lot of people lost their fortune, but not Mr. Soros who took advantage of the situation. In this article, we made a list of signs indicating that it’s time to short sell.

The first and the best indicator is a downward trend. Naturally, it’s not so obvious sometimes. Traders usually use a set of technical indicators, like a “moving average” that helps to filter out noise factors and see the real trend.

Before the market falls there are warning signs that the crisis is coming. For example, if a public company misses its quarterly earnings estimates, it’s a clear sign of a declining trend for its stocks. The management of this company will make a press release or a conference call to explain the situation to investors. On the basis of it, the Wall Street will make a report to its brokers to reduce their positions. If you were lucky to sell short between the moment of a press release and the broker’s report, eventually you will earn some profit.

Another red flag to short-sell is when you noticed insider selling in large quantities. Surely, sometimes it is profit-driven in the moment, but it may be a good sign of a more serious downfall. Of course, companies close to the bankruptcy are perfect candidates to short sell successfully. Nevertheless, you can short sell if the company is declining gross margins, increasing inventories and accounts receivable.

To check out whether the company is facing some problems, do not hesitate to make phone calls or write emails to the company’s customers and suppliers.

To sum up, successful short selling is all about timing. Follow these tips, hone your trading skills and you might earn profits just like legendary millionaires.
