Bollinger Bands

This strategy is one of the most frequently used in online trading and has established itself as an effective tool for CFD’s. Bollinger Bands suggests a time when to open sell or buy positions by displaying two color bands: blue upper and red lower. The trader should open the buy position when asset price touches the lower band and the sell position in the opposite case. The tool is great for beginners due to its relative simplicity.

RSI (Relative Strength Indicator)

RSI compares markets and their belonging to bulls or bears. It is quite easy: being based on the rule of thumb, it indicated when you should open a position. If the RSI line goes above 30 point, the trader is recommended to open a buy position. If the RSI line runs below 70 point, it is better to open a sell position. This instrument is considered to be another great helper for the novice trader.

MACD Moving Average Convergence Divergence

The MACD strategy is perfect for someone who is entering the CFDs trading world because of its high approachability and great reputation. It was invented in 1979 and is used for following trends of both CFD online and Forex. The MACD looks like a chart with two moving averages and a histogram with additional options for experienced traders who can develop their own strategy on the base of this one.

Fibonacci Target Zones

This tool indicates the lowest and highest points of a trend (bullish or bearish) within defined period of time. The fib lines on the trading chart help you guess whether to open buy and sell position by predicting a result of asset’s behavior after its move in either direction. Fibonacci target zones strategy is also effective for setting a loss and taking profit in one or another case.

ParSAR

This strategy is also called a parabolic SAR and represents a technical indicator showing momentum and predicting its direction changes. ParSAR is widely used in combination with MACD to see more trading opportunities. The shortcoming of this tool is a possible partial malfunction in case of certain market conditions (especially the asset whipsaw).
